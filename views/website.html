Website Portfolio Breakdown

I wanted my website interface to have a tone and layout that matched my personality and design inspiration. Besides having some fun with the monochrome and blue colors (colors of clothes that I tend to wear), I wanted to make sure the navigation through the site was easy and prioritized the projects that I have made.

The most significant changes I planned to make for the second model of my website were:

    1. Landing page: adding the project to the front page (less clicks to the most important aspect of the website)

    2. Internal housing: originally my case studies were housed on a third party app, which decreases UX

    3. Web Flow: I wanted to present information in an hour glass format (general information --> specific (project work) --> back to general). I find this UI display to be most effective to provide a cohesive entry and exit out of information processing.
